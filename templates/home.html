This is a debated medical condition. Many refusing to believe in its existance at all. From my personal experience I have had a room full of Dermatologists diagnose me with RSS and in stark contrast others will tell me it is absolute nonsence and will snigger at the diagnosis. This is evendently a condition that polarizes opinion amongst doctors.
This page is a personal account of what I have experinced and continue to do so. Nothing here is intended to diagnose anyone. But it is my honest held belief that something went seriously wrong with my skin. I have built this page to both create awareness and share personal testimony. Below I have tried to give a glance from sources that are also creating awareness to give you an overview of what it is.

Rapaport is a leading voice in raising awareness of this disease. I believe he is the Dr who originally discovered the problem. He argues that the symptom set is different from normal eczema patients and can be identified uniquely through patients presenting with 'red, itchy and burning skin'. He has a blog and also provides tele medical support to people with this disease. I have personally used this service, although I could not continue due to finacial reasons.
He has an exstensive blog you can visit using the link below.

The National Eczema Association is now also raising awareness of this disease. They list the following debilitating symptoms that are associated with this condition. Symptoms such as 'burning, weeping, flaking, shedding, peeling, spreading, swelling, redness, wrinkling, thin skin, pus-filled bumps, cracking, itching, nodules, pain, insomnia, hair loss, shivering, fatigue, depression and disability'. Whilst they acknowledge that the sysmptoms do not always present like this many personal accounts that I have read would support this to be accurate, as indeed would I from my own experience.

- Hewett, L. (2022) Topical Steroid Withdrawal: What the Eczema Community Needs to Know, Now. Available at: https://nationaleczema.org/blog/tsw-need-to-know/.

ITSAN which is an acronym for International Topical Steroid Addiction Network was established to raise awareness and support people suffering with the condition. They describe the condition in the following way "Topical Steroid Withdrawal Syndrome (TSWS), also known as Topical Steroid Addiction (TSA) or Red Skin Syndrome (RSS), is a debilitating condition that can arise from the use of topical steroids to treat a skin problem, such as eczema."

- What is TSW Syndrome? (no date). Available at: https://www.itsan.org/what-is-tsw-syndrome/.
